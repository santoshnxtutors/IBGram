# IBGram Brand Voice

## Tone

IBGram should sound warm, academically serious, premium and practical. The voice is closer to an experienced academic advisor than a startup pitch. It should help parents feel safe and help students feel understood.

## Allowed Phrases

- IB and IGCSE tutoring matched to your subject, level and goals.
- Tutors who understand school timelines, weak areas and assessment requirements.
- Support for PYP, MYP, DP and IGCSE students.
- Online, home and hybrid tutoring where available.
- Practice and planning tools for the work between tutor sessions.
- Availability depends on subject, schedule, location and tutoring mode.
- IB Gram is an independent tutoring platform.

## Avoided Phrases

- World's best
- Gold standard
- Dominating
- Dramatically boost
- Top 1%
- Elite educator, unless verified by a real internal standard
- Guaranteed result
- Perfect score promise
- Proprietary AI
- Secret weapon
- Game changer
- Instant guaranteed match

## CTA Style

- Find a tutor for my child
- Book a free consultation
- Check tutor availability
- Share your subject requirement
- Explore IB tutor options
- Explore IB subjects
- See how matching works
- Talk to an academic advisor

Avoid CTAs such as "Join Member", "Unlock Excellence", "Supercharge", "Dominate" and "Get Started Now" when a more specific next step is available.

## Parent-Facing Wording

Parents need clarity, trust and realistic next steps. Use words such as requirement, schedule, subject level, learning need, communication, availability, tutor fit and first session.

Example:

"Share your child's curriculum, subject, level, timeline and preferred tutoring mode. IB Gram will help you review suitable tutor options."

## Student-Facing Wording

Students need language that respects pressure without overpromising. Use words such as weak areas, revision plan, exam practice, IA direction, confidence, feedback and routine.

Example:

"Use practice sets and revision checklists to stay clearer about what to work on before the next tutor session."

## AI-Tool Wording

AI should sound supportive, not magical. Prefer:

- Study planner
- Practice paper builder
- Weak-area tracker
- Revision checklist
- Rubric-aware feedback, only if actually supported

Avoid implying that AI can replace teaching, guarantee marks or predict real exam questions.

## Tutor-Matching Wording

Tutor matching should sound thoughtful and human. Mention subject, level, programme, schedule, locality, mode and learning need. Avoid making the process sound fully automated or instant in every case.

Example:

"Matching considers subject fit, level, preferred mode, schedule and the kind of support the student needs."

## Proof And Testimonial Wording

Use proof only when it is backed by real data. For placeholder testimonials, keep language modest and outcome-safe.

Good:

"The tutor helped us structure revision and communicate next steps clearly."

Avoid:

"I went from a 4 to a 7 because of IB Gram."

## Claim Safety Rules

- No guaranteed grades.
- No invented review counts.
- No fake school partnerships.
- No unsupported university admission claims.
- No exact tutor counts unless maintained by real data.
- Add availability caveats where tutor, city or mode coverage may vary.
